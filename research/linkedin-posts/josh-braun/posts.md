**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josh-braun_josh-a-prospect-responded-to-my-cold-email-activity-7074138982707556352-T9Cm

A prospect responded to my cold email saying they are not interested.
Here is what NOT to do in your follow up:

"I haven't decided yet if I'm going to give up completely, but this is it for now."

That passive aggressive approach never works.

Here is what CAN work instead:

"Hey, I know timing might not be great. Or maybe what we do is not a fit for your company.
Or — and I can take the criticism — I did a pretty poor job in your inbox.
My plan is to work on my outreach and circle back in 3 months to see if things are different."

The best thing that can happen: you get real feedback.
If you were compelling, they would have replied during your sequence.
Take the feedback to heart.

Your prospects owe you nothing.

## Post 2
**Date:** 2024
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/josh-braun

One of the most useful sales ideas I borrowed did not come from sales.
It came from intelligence gathering. John Nolan called it elicitation.

The principle: People do not like feeling examined.

The moment you ask: "How important is this?" their brain knows it is being evaluated.
So they become careful. Measured. Polite.

But when you make an observation instead:
"Sounds like this is not a big priority."

Something different happens. Now you are no longer asking them to answer.
You are inviting them to react.

And humans are wired to react — especially when they think you have misunderstood them.

Use observations, not questions, in your cold outreach to spark real conversations.

The complete cold email framework that actually works in 2025:

Most people fail at cold email because they make it about themselves.
Here is how to flip that:

**Step 1 — Research First (5 minutes per prospect)**
- Find one specific thing about their company or role
- Check their LinkedIn activity, company news, or job postings
- Use that as your opening line — not "I hope this finds you well"

**Step 2 — Write the Email**
- Line 1: Personalized observation about THEM
- Line 2: The specific problem you solve
- Line 3: One sentence of social proof
- Line 4: A simple yes/no question as your CTA

Total length: under 75 words

**Step 3 — Follow Up**
- Follow up at least 4-5 times
- Each follow up adds a new angle or piece of value
- Never just say "just checking in"

**The mindset shift:**
Stop thinking about closing a sale.
Start thinking about starting a conversation.
The sale comes later.

How to use LinkedIn for B2B outreach in 2025:

LinkedIn is the highest intent B2B channel available right now.
Here is how to use it properly:

**Connection Request (under 300 characters):**
→ Reference something specific — a post they wrote, a mutual connection,
  or their company milestone
→ Never pitch in the connection request
→ Your only goal is to get them to accept

**First Message after connecting:**
→ Thank them briefly
→ Ask one genuine question about their work
→ Still no pitch

**Second or Third Message:**
→ Now you can share what you do
→ Keep it to 2-3 sentences
→ End with a soft CTA — not "book a 30 minute call"
  but "would it be worth a quick 10 minute chat?"

Key insight: LinkedIn InMail gets 10-25% response rates
vs cold email at 1-5%.

But most people ruin it by pitching too early.
Warm the relationship first — then pitch.
